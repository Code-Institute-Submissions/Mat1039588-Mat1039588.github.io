Biography and Style



Eric Clapton is with no doubt one of the most important guitar players ever lived. The British guitarist is an acclaimed musician starting at a very young age in south London. He was born in March 1945 in England. It was during the 1965 that Clapton joined the Bluesbrakers and John Mayall and they recorded one of the most influential hard blues album, The BluesBrakers with Eric Clapton (also known as the Beano Album). The international success and fame came after leaving the Bluesbrakers, Clapton joined what later became one of the most influential rock band ever: Cream. With Ginger Baker at the drums and Jack Bruce playing the bass guitar, the three young British boys influenced with their sound generations and generations of musicians. The 70s have been a bit of a black hole for Clapton. The wild misuse of drugs and alcohol, made him lose the path he was destined to pursue. After more than 10 years, he managed to come back to life and take control of his life again. Ranked amongst the best guitar players of all time, inducted in the Rock an Roll Hall Of Fame three times: with the yardbirds, with Cream and as a solo artist.
Eric Clapton is considered a master of the blues guitar, particularly of the Chicago blues. His technique and sound make him immediately recognisable. Phrasing and feel with the instrument unique and hard to replicate. Here are few peculiarities that makes Eric Clapton sounds like no other: As a blues player, the minor pentatonic looks like the obvious choice for a guitar player or any blue player. To give the song a more exotic sound, Clapton often uses the major pentatonic. The loud amplifier. Having a cranked up amp will give you just what you need. Tube amps are the best choice because of the tubes. Once properly warm, they will have tons and tons of warm and thick classic overdrives tones. He just turn the amp volume all the way up. Know as “Slowhand”, Clapton became famous for his phrasing. He never played just random notes or just for the sake of it. It is important for him that the music serves the song. His extremely melodic phrasing makes him one of those few able to creative guitar solos so well connected that fits in the song. People think about him as a master in “composing a song inside a song”. His solos always have a beginning, a middle part and an ending. Since the 70s he’s always been inclined to play Fender guitars. His signature Fender Stratocaster has the peculiarity of having a mid boost circuit inside its body that allows him to have a booster of mid frequencies whenever he needs and going back to the initial amount of volume just turning a knob on his guitar. Blues music had always been an obsession for Clapton. He became a master of the genre because he deeply studied and analysed the blues going back to its roots and presenting it in a way, he would be easily recognisable by just listening to few notes and sharing a music full of history, tradition, passion.
